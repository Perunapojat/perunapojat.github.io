Welcome to Farrion & The Provinces

      Farrion

      • The Provinces

The border marches are a contested region between the VG and the Kingdom of Chevalier. The region is a mess of semi-independent marcher lords that internaly vye for power, all while the bordering powers try to bring the marchers to swear allegiance to them.

Due to being situated between the VG and Chevalria the border marches has been a melting pot of both cultures. The majority of the population consists of humans, but there are significant minorities of elves, half-elves, gnomes, dwarves and kobolds. Almost everyone follows the Farrion Pantheon escept for a few kobolds and dwarves that rather follow their ancestral beliefs.

Ængmark is the strongest and largest kingdom in the region of Pryddain. Separated from the rest of Farrion by a mountainrange the area is among the most recently settled on the continent. The kingdom was founded by a adventuring expedition from Gormark that provided protection to early settlers.

Ængmark and Pryddain in general is less cosmopolitan than the rest of Farrion. The region has to a large extent only two races, humans and dwarves. The Farrion Pantheon is the majority faith but the Dwarven Pantheon is also legally recognized in the country.

Following the collapse of the Precursor Empire, the small whaling colony Gnommoport became independent and formed the first Gnomish State waging wars of conquest on the neighbouring regions. However the first Gnomish State was stopped by a coalition of nations and heroes ending their rampant expansionism. What followed was a dissarmed Gnomish Kingdom and supervised by the victorious coalition. The kingdom was highly unpopular amongst the gnomes leading to the seccond Gnomish State after the self defence forces couped the king and dismissed the dearmament treaty. The resurgent Gnomish state managed to conquer some lost regions but was once again defeated by a coalition of nations. The state was once again dissolved but the peace treaty was less harsh letting Gnommoria keep its northern conquests by letting the Gormans settle on the formerly gnomish southern coast. Since then the Gnomes have left their expansionist ambitions behind them and instead focusing on whaling and trading

Gnommoria is the only gnomish state in the world. However, due to their warmongering and discriminatory past they have gotten a reputation as being racist, scammers and schemers. Trough their conquests they have spread their religion of Gnimto to the entire western coast of The Provinces, bar the former Gorman territories and Gormark. Obviously Gnommoria is a gnomish majority state but there exist enclaves of human and elven settlements within their realm.

The Imperium was formed by the conquistador expeditions that were the first explorers on the Kobold continent after Farriels discovery of the new world. The Sermano people as they were known in The Provinces were forced to relocate to Farrion after attacks from nomads grew more and more. Following their exodus to the new shores of Farrion they started conquering the kobolds to the west, eventually relocating their capital to the former kobold metropolis Faxotl, renaming it Roxico city.

The empire consists of a caste society based on race, with the numerous kobolds working as a servile race while humans and elves make up the military, state and middle class. The Farrion Pantheon originated largely from the pre voyage beliefs of the Sermano and as such the empire sees itself as the harbringer of the divine.

Carreg Bád is a dwarven realm on Farrion and the sole dwarven nation with a navy. The Carregi pride themselves for their great maritime skill, but they are a empire in decline as their mountainsides have become bare of any trees.

Carreg Bád is a purely dwarven civilization founded by dwarven migranting to Farrion. All dwarves proudly follow the ancient Dwarven Pantheon.

Chevalria, the land of knights and honor, of court-life and aristocracy, and most importantly the nation of Tarrot. The chivalrous kingdom is only matched by the greed of the VG and the might of the Empire. Although the kingdom is made up of mostly human subjects it is ruled by a half-elven and high elven court.

Although ruled by elves the kingdom is dominantly human with dwarven, halfling and gnomish minorities intermingling with the humans and elves. They follow the Farrion Pantheon.

Eliá was founded after the Grecho fled over the ocean to their new home. Having lived a peaceful existence on the western coast of The Provinces they were eventually attacked by the ever expanding nomads. The survivors of the attack fled to sea where they were rescued by a halfling trade cog headed for Farrion. The Grecho embraced the religion and culture of the halflings and thus became the Eliá. Feeling endebted to the halflings the Eliá promised to protect their sovereignty on Farrion.

Being situated at the mouth to Farrion, Eliá has become a cosmopolitan metropolis of races and customs. However Eliá is mostly human with large minorities of halflings. Although they speak common they tend to write with the halfling alphabet often leading to confusion when communicating trough text. The Eliáns follow the Halfling Pantheon.

The Precursor Empire didn't survive the fall of the gemstone cities. However, two dynaties claim to be the rightfull succesor to the Precursors, the Kim dynasty and the Xheng dynasty. The dragon-elf Kim dynasty controls much of the north while the human Xheng dynasty holds the fertile south. The two rival contenders are militarily at a stalemate having lead to a conflict in limbo as they both try to undermine the power of the other.

The Empire while split in two is otherwise quite homogenous with a large population of humans and a elite of dragon-elves. However the two contender nations differ in religious thought; the Kim dynasty has created a cult of worship surrounding their empress and a philosophy of self dependence called Yuche. Meanwhile the Xheng enjoy a secular state where ancestor worship, Way of Roc and Dragon, and the temple of the Ground Mother all exist side by side.

Dragoi was founded only shortly before Ængmark by a harassed minority within Gormark, the Celto. They escaped across the northern coast of Farrion following the star ofd the red dragon until they found unsettled land west of Moorthìr.

As the rest of Pryddain, Dragoi is inhabited mostly by humans, however unlike the other Pryddanic nations there exist enclaves of Wood-elf forest settlements mostly hidden from the rest of the world. The Dragonians follow the Farrion Pantheon.

The Enchanted Woods lie at the foot of the western mountain range. It is a region alive with magic and inhabited by woods-elves that protect it from intruders.

Little is known about the inhabitants of the forest, but it's suspected that they worship nature spirits in the so called Tree Pact.

Land of the dry heat and of salt, only the hardy bedouins manage to live outside of the oasises. Almost all of the former olive plantations have dried and shriveled up following the ever increasing heat on the dry island.

Gonria is the only realm that consists of a majority half-elven population.

Gormark is the only surviving Gorman realm on The Provinces. Being constantly harassed by nomads hasn't changed their mind on staying on The Provinces instead of fleeing to Farrion like the rest of the Gormans.

The most common races in Gormark are humans, gnomes, half-elves and high elves. The Farrion Pantheon is the most common religion but there are also adherents to Gnimto and a few adherents to the Way of Roc and Dragon.

Íarn Morthwyl, or the Iron Hammer in common, is the most significant dwarven civilization on Farrion. They are mostly isolationists with little interest in the world outside their mountainhalls instead focusing inwards and downwards digging ever deeper and grander halls below the earth.

There are almost no other race than the dwarves within the mountainhalls. Although the dwarves follow their ancient Dwarven Pantheon, there exists a small minority of new thinkers that have instead abandoned their old beliefs in favor of the Farrion Pantheon.

The halflings on Farrion came originally from Putázo. On Farrion they formed small farming communities where they live idyllic lives unbothered by the world at large. Thanks to the Eliná they have lived in peace with no need to defend themselves. As such their communities seem completely anarchic to outsiders as there is no sovereign, instead all the shires govern themselves and solve local issues by voting on them.

Although the shires are almost purely halfling, they are tolerant of the other races giving refuge to those in need as long as they don't cause trouble. The main faith among the halflings is their own Halfling Pantheon but there are some that have converted to the Farrion Pantheon.

Kayl and Jamal are both sultanates that are founded by Gonrian bedouins. The lands are so harsh in the desert that only they can manage to thrive under such harsh conditions.

The sultanate consists mostly of human and half-elven communities.

Moorthìr is the first of the Pryddanic realms to be settled. Like the Dragoi it is settled by a harassed minority that fled to the new virgin lands of Farrion. Due to the harsh conditions of the moors and the constant skirmishes with Norda and Ængmark the Moorthìr highlanders have become fighters of legendary skill.

Like the rest of Pryddain, Moorthìr is almost purely human. They follow the Farrion Pantheon.

Langbet was founded as a monastic order hidden away in the mountains by the "Lone Wolf" Du Lang. Du Lang taught his students the Way of Roc and Dragon, a philosophy on how to view and approach the world. Langbet has lived in peaceful isolation hidden among the mountains, but that hasn't stopped them from getting involved in combating evil and tyranny in The Provinces. Their most hailed achievement being defeating the Gnomish state twice in battle despite being outnumbered.

Langbet is the place where eastern monks go to learn martial arts. All of the orders adherents follow either the way of the Roc or the way of the Dragon and the order is open to all races.

The ancient lands of Mjwgypt didn't know of the outside world before adventurers from The Provinces started invading the lands of Farrion. Although the kobolds became subjugated beneath these foreigners the Sphinxes(tabaxi) of Mjwgypt avoided that fate. Being isolated by desert to the east, west and south, and having the inner sea to the north, the Sphinxes have lived in isolation.

The Sphinxes of Mjwgypt follow the sun goddess Kii'Ra whose mortal vessel is the Pharao.

The cold harsh lands of the north have shaped a people with souls just as cold. The infamous norda is not one people or one nation but a collective term for the tribal raiders from the north that descend upon coastal settelment looking for loot. They are accomplished seamen that discovered Fjorðaland. They got the nickname "viking" after raiding a Gorman bay monastery(Vik-kloister).

The Norda faith(Norda duality) is dualistic and seems paradoxical to outsiders. The norda view the world as consisting of two natures: The All-Father and The Great Ettin. They divide the opposite aspects of the world between the two natures, such as fire belonging to The All-Father and ice to The Great Ettin. The central concept is however the duality of the world as the Norda view everything as containing both natures. There can't be life without death and there can't be death without life. As such norda morality is seen as weird among outsiders since they tolerate necromancers and reject tjhe existence of pure evil and pure good.

The dwarves of Osthalr live on the very edge of civilization on The Provinces. Surrounded by the taiga and the cold they have made a fortress of solitude out of the eastern mountainrange. They are the least populuos dwarven civilization in the world but nhave achieved great wealth by having a monopoly on bitiminous coal.

They follow the ancient Dwarven Pantheon.

Orłow was settled by a company of Sibrerian fortune seekers trying to escape their miserable lives in Sibrir. They settled on the north coast after seeing a giant white eagle, thus becoming the Eagle kingdom. Orłow is seen as a cursed land in the rest of Farrion as the land is filled with horrible monstrous creatures that that terrorize the population. However there have been recent developments in hunting down the monsters after a group of monster hunters was formed.

The people of Orłow are suspicious of foreigners and as such non humans are mistrusted and discriminated in Orłow. They follow the Farrion Pantheon.

Putázo used to be the center of wine and entertainment during the Precursor Empire. It hasn't changed much in the present day as the homeland of halflings and of philosophy and entertainment continues to thrive.

Putázo is the land of origin for the halflings, but many rich foreigners have settled down on the paradise island where wine is as common as water. The halflings folllow the Halfling Pantheon.

Sibrir and Rusrir are desolate lands filled with taiga forests and bad soil that barely even manages to feed the people. In the south the Sibrir boyars bicker amongst themselves waging petty wars and treating their subjects harshly. In the north the tribe of Rusrir lead by a norda chief plans their southward expansion down the river.

People of many races suffer under the cold trees of the taiga, but chief amongst them are the numerous humans that outnumber all other races. The Sibrir are faithful worshipers of the Ground Mother, while the sibrir under the Rusrir chief follow the enigmatic Cult of Rusrik.

Rødmark is a norda adventurer settlement on the northern coast of Farrion. The norda settlers have taken on cultural influences from the conquered locals, abandoning their warrior existance for a life of farming and peace. However their presence is seen as a treath by the VG which still sees them as despicable barbarians.

The norda settlers have created a melting pot of culture in Rødmark. While many still follow the beliefs of the Duality others have begun embracing the local Farrion Pantheon.

S'ralia used to be a penal colony during the Precursor Empire. But with the fall of the empire, the former prisoners had to fend for themselves on a hostile continent. S'ralian wildlife is amongst the most dangerous in the world, and as such the S'ralians have to be hardy and dangerous themselves to survive. The settlements on S'ralia are all coastal fortresses able to endure any gigaspider or mega-emu attack.

Due to the penal history of S'ralia many of the inhabitants are greenskins or have orc blood in their veins. People from all walks of faith live among the fortress communities living a life of danger.

The VG or the Vereintige Graafschaften is a confederation of duchies. Encompassing many cultures and races the confederacy is a loose union that works together for economic growth. The VG is ruled by a council of guilds that rules from the island city of Roosenstadt, the council however lets local mayors and dukes rule their realms as they see fit so long as they don't disturbe the actions of the guilds and pay their taxes.

Due to the cosmopolitan nature of the confederacy there exist pretty much communities of all sorts of races within it, such as the goliath Marienlanders of the eastern coast. The union is united in one thing though and that is the common language of Bundessprach, which is more common than common within the VG. The majority of the population follows the Farrion Pantheon.

Tlacocol, the last remnant of the kobold realms. They are seen as uncivilized by the other Farrion nations due to the practice of cannibalism and living sacrifice.

The kobolds of the remnant empire don't speak common as they try to preserve their cultural identity from the colonial invaders. They worship strange gods via blood rituals, ritual cannibalism and living sacrifice.

Vesthalr is the oldest dwarven nation in the world. Thanks to the massive copper deposits in the western mountainrange they have become among the richest nations of The Provinces. Lately the veins have been running dry and the clans are looking for possibilities to prospect for ore deeper down.

The western mountainrange is the founding point for much of dwarven culture aswell as the Dwarven Pantheon. Among the mountainhalls live not only dwarves but also some humans that sought refuge among the mountainhomes from the thundering hordes of nomads.

Yii Ha is among the youngest nations on The Provinces, but their culture is as old as the Empire. They broke free from the yoke of the Xheng dynasty thanks to backing from the Kim dynasty. Much of the lands of Yii Ha are arid an as such the main source of income comes from raising cattle. The Yii Ha hold weapons dear to their cultural identity and they have gone as far as making it required by law to be armed at all times.

The biggest faith among the Yii Ha is Gnimto spirituality, but there are also some that still worship their ancestors.

Wizra is a small realm neighbouring two military giants. They have cleverly avoided annexation through a most clever trick, banking. The entire country has become a fortress of wealth as they safekeep the money of wealthy merchants and kings alike. Thanks to this clever scheeme they have become renowned for their apparent neutrality in all neighbouring conflicts.

Although common is the most common langauge in Wizra, there are som migrants from the VG that have formed small communities that speak Bundessprach. The Wizra hold the Farrion Pantheon as their state religion.

Kayl and Jamal are both sultanates that are founded by Gonrian bedouins. The lands are so harsh in the desert that only they can manage to thrive under such harsh conditions.

The sultanate consists mostly of human and half-elven communities.

The Hallowed Isle is inhabited not by humans but by gnomes and elves. The south of the island is mostly plains where the afermentioned gnomes live. They are famed for their ginger and merry apperance and often clothed in green. Meanwhile on the northern part of the island the wood-elves have made their domain amongst the forests.
